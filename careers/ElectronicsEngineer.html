About Dirac Labs

Dirac Labs is revolutionizing navigation with cutting-edge technologies designed to be robust against blocking and spoofing. By integrating advanced quantum sensors with machine learning, Dirac Labs accurately determines position using the Earth’s magnetic field—offering a reliable, precise, and independent positioning solution.

Dirac Labs is an early-stage company emerging out of quantum sensing and nanophotonics research efforts at the University of Wisconsin-Madison (USA). It has won the prestigious US-India Endowment Fund’s award to develop quantum sensors, and is supported by Creative Destruction Labs, Emergent Ventures, and NASA.

About the Role

You will be a part of a small, fast-paced, research-focused team developing electronics for real-time positioning and navigation systems using multi-sensor data fusion. Responsibilities include:

  • Design and develop electronics for quantum sensors (inertial and electromagnetic).
  • Integrate and test performance of sensors on mobile platforms.
  • Collaborate with quantum physicists & machine learning scientists to develop a robust hardware architecture for sensing and real-time data processing.

Requirements (Must-Haves)

  • A Bachelor’s degree in electrical, electronics or instrumentation engineering (or a related field) with at least 1+ year of relevant experience.
  • Knowledge of hardware description languages (e.g., Verilog).
  • Experience with FPGA design tools (e.g. Vivado) and HDL Simulation Tools.
  • Past projects involving programming in C, C++ or Python.
  • Experience with test & measurement equipment: oscilloscopes, spectrum analyzers, etc.
  • Strong debugging and analytical skills.

Bonus Points For

  • Past projects involving RF or microwave circuits.
  • Interest in ASIC development.
  • Hands-on experience with hardware integration or sensor calibration.
  • Past projects involving inertial sensing: attitude, heading, and reference systems (AHRS) development, robotics controllers, flight computers, etc.
  • Experience in mixed-signal circuit development and analysis.
